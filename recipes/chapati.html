Chapati Recipe

Description

These are soft, flaky, and layered flatbreads, perfect for pairing with beef stew, lentils (kamande), or tea.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour: 2 cups (or 1 cup white + 1 cup brown/atta flour)
  • Boiling water: 1 cup (must be hot for softness)
  • Cooking oil/Ghee: 3-4 tablespoons (plus extra for brushing)
  • Sugar: 1/2 teaspoon (optional, for browning)
  • Salt: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Optional: 1/2 cup milk (for extra softness)

Steps

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and sugar
  2. Add Oil & Water: Add 1 tablespoon of oil to the flour and mix with your hands until it feels like sand. Gradually add the boiling water (and milk if using) while mixing with a wooden spoon to form a soft, elastic dough
  3. Knead: Place the dough on a floured surface and knead for 5â€“10 minutes until smooth.
  4. Rest: Cover the dough with a damp cloth or cling wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  5. Divide & Shape (Layering): Divide the dough into 8-10 equal balls. Roll each ball out into a thin circle, brush with oil, and roll it up like a pipe, then coil it into a cinnamon-roll shape. Flatten slightly with your palm and roll out again into a 10-12 inch circle.
  6. Cook: Heat a heavy pan (tava) over medium-high heat. Place the rolled-out chapati on the hot pan and turn it after a few seconds, even without oil, to let it bubble up.
  7. Oil & Finish: Apply 1 teaspoon of oil on both sides, turning frequently until golden brown spots appear and it is fully cooked.
  8. Store: Place cooked chapatis in a covered container or hot pot to keep them soft.
