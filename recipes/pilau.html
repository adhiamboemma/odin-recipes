Pilau Recipe

Description

Swahili beef pilau is a fragrant, one-pot rice dish made by browning beef, sautéing onions, garlic, ginger, and aromatic spices (cumin, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, black pepper). It often includes potatoes and is cooked with rice in meat broth until fluffy. Serve hot with kachumbari (onion-tomato salad).

Ingredients

  • Meat: 500g - 1kg beef, cubed
  • Rice: 3 cups Basmati rice, soaked for 30 mins
  • Spices: 1 tbsp whole pilau masala (cumin, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, black pepper)
  • Aromatics: 2 large onions (sliced), 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Liquid: 3-4 cups beef broth or water

Steps

  1. Prepare Meat: Boil beef until tender. If using potatoes, add them to the broth to soften.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil/ghee in a large pot, fry onions until dark brown (key for color). Add ginger-garlic paste and whole spices, cooking until fragrant.
  3. Combine: Add boiled beef to the spices, stirring for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Cook Rice: Add washed rice and broth/water (use a 1:1.5 ratio of rice to water). Season with salt.
  5. Simmer & Steam: Bring to a simmer until liquid reduces. Cover with a tight lid, reduce heat to low, and steam for 15-20 minutes.
  6. Serve: Fluff with a fork and serve with kachumbari.
